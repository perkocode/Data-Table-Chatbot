Row ID: A unique index or row number for each record.

Order ID: A unique identifier for each individual order.

Order Date: The date the order was placed by the customer.

Ship Date: The date the order was shipped from the warehouse.

Ship Mode: The method of shipping selected for the order (e.g., Standard Class, First Class, Second Class, Same Day).

Customer ID: A unique identifier for each customer.

Customer Name: The full name of the customer who placed the order.

Segment: The market segment the customer belongs to (e.g., Consumer, Corporate, Home Office).

Country: The country where the customer is located.

City: The city where the customer is located.

State: The state or province where the customer is located.

Postal Code: The postal or ZIP code for the customer’s location.

Region: The broader geographical region where the customer is located (e.g., East, West, Central, South).

Product ID: A unique identifier for each product in the catalog.

Category: The high-level product category (e.g., Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology).

Sub-Category: A more specific classification within a category (e.g., Chairs, Phones, Binders).

Product Name: The name or title of the product.

Sales: The total revenue from the sale of the product (calculated as Quantity × Unit Price minus Discount).

Quantity: The number of units sold in the transaction.

Discount: The percentage discount applied to the sale (e.g., 0.2 represents 20% off).

Profit: The profit generated from the transaction (Sales minus Cost).

Shipping Cost: The cost incurred to ship the order.

Order Priority: The level of urgency for fulfilling the order (e.g., Low, Medium, High, Critical).

Market: The sales market or region in which the order was placed (e.g., APAC, EU, US).

Customer Segment: Alternate labeling for "Segment" (some versions include both fields).

Year: The year extracted from the order date (used for time series analysis).

Month: The month extracted from the order date (used for trend analysis).
