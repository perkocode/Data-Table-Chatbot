Term: EBITDA
Definition: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A measure of a company's profitability from operations, used to compare profitability between companies and industries.

Term: Net Present Value (NPV)
Definition: The value of future cash flows discounted back to the present using a discount rate. A core concept in capital budgeting.

Term: Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
Definition: The discount rate that makes the net present value of a project zero. Commonly used to evaluate investment opportunities.

Term: Return on Equity (ROE)
Definition: Measures profitability by comparing net income to shareholder equity. Indicates how effectively a company is using equity financing.

Term: Market Capitalization
Definition: The total market value of a company’s outstanding shares. Calculated as share price × number of shares outstanding.

Term: Price to Earnings Ratio (P/E)
Definition: A valuation ratio calculated as market price per share divided by earnings per share (EPS). Reflects market expectations of earnings growth.

Term: Working Capital
Definition: Current assets minus current liabilities. Indicates a company’s short-term liquidity and operational efficiency.

Term: Break-Even Point
Definition: The sales level at which total revenues equal total costs, resulting in neither profit nor loss.

Term: Operating Leverage
Definition: A measure of how fixed costs affect profits. High operating leverage means small changes in revenue can cause large changes in operating income.

Term: SWOT Analysis
Definition: A strategic planning tool used to identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats related to business competition or project planning.

Term: Porter's Five Forces
Definition: A framework for analyzing competitive forces: threat of new entrants, threat of substitutes, bargaining power of buyers, bargaining power of suppliers, and competitive rivalry.

Term: Balanced Scorecard
Definition: A performance measurement framework that includes financial and non-financial metrics across four perspectives: financial, customer, internal processes, and learning & growth.

Term: Strategic Business Unit (SBU)
Definition: A semi-autonomous unit within a larger company, focused on a specific market, product, or service line.

Term: Competitive Advantage
Definition: An attribute that allows a company to outperform its competitors, such as cost leadership, differentiation, or niche focus.

Term: Capital Expenditure (CapEx)
Definition: Funds used by a company to acquire, upgrade, or maintain physical assets such as property, buildings, or equipment.

Term: Operating Expense (OpEx)
Definition: Day-to-day business expenses not tied to the production of goods or services, such as rent, utilities, and salaries.

Term: Lean Management
Definition: An approach focused on reducing waste and increasing efficiency in business processes, often used in operations and manufacturing.

Term: Just-in-Time (JIT)
Definition: Inventory management strategy aimed at reducing carrying costs by receiving goods only as they are needed.

Term: Net Working Capital
Definition: A liquidity metric that indicates whether a company has enough short-term assets to cover its short-term liabilities.

Term: Customer Segmentation
Definition: The process of dividing a customer base into groups based on common characteristics to better tailor marketing strategies.

Term: Brand Equity
Definition: The commercial value derived from consumer perception of the brand name, rather than the product or service itself.

Term: Product Lifecycle
Definition: The stages a product goes through from introduction and growth to maturity and decline.

Term: Churn Rate
Definition: The percentage of customers who stop doing business with a company over a specific period.

Term: Economies of Scale
Definition: Cost advantages that a business obtains due to expansion, typically measured by cost per unit of output.

Term: Marginal Cost
Definition: The cost of producing one additional unit of a product.

Term: Variable Cost
Definition: Costs that change in proportion to production output, such as raw materials or direct labor.

Term: Fixed Cost
Definition: Costs that remain constant regardless of output level, such as rent or salaries.

Term: Cash Flow Statement
Definition: A financial statement that shows how changes in balance sheet and income accounts affect cash and cash equivalents.

Term: Accrual Accounting
Definition: An accounting method where revenue and expenses are recorded when they are earned or incurred, not when cash is exchanged.

Term: Corporate Governance
Definition: The system of rules, practices, and processes by which a company is directed and controlled.

Term: Risk Management
Definition: The process of identifying, assessing, and controlling threats to an organization's capital and earnings.

Term: Supply Chain
Definition: The network between a company and its suppliers to produce and distribute a specific product.

Term: Stakeholder
Definition: Any party with an interest in a company, including shareholders, employees, customers, suppliers, and the community.

Term: Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
Definition: The total market value of all goods and services produced within a country in a given time period.

Term: Inflation
Definition: The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, eroding purchasing power.

Term: Monetary Policy
Definition: The process by which a central bank manages money supply and interest rates to control inflation and stabilize the economy.

Term: Fiscal Policy
Definition: Government decisions about taxation and spending to influence the economy.

Term: Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)
Definition: A business model in which companies integrate social and environmental concerns into their operations and stakeholder interactions.

Term: KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
Definition: A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively a company is achieving key business objectives.

Term: SaaS (Software as a Service)
Definition: A cloud-based service where instead of downloading software, users access it via the internet, often through a subscription.

Term: Burn Rate
Definition: The rate at which a company uses up its cash reserves, typically measured in startups.

Term: Gross Margin
Definition: Revenue minus cost of goods sold, expressed as a percentage of revenue. Indicates how efficiently a company is producing goods.

Term: Forecasting
Definition: The process of making predictions about future performance based on historical data and analytical models.

Term: Scenario Planning
Definition: A strategic planning method used to make flexible long-term plans by considering possible future events or situations.

Term: Blue Ocean Strategy
Definition: A business approach that seeks to create uncontested market space and make the competition irrelevant.

Term: Business Model Canvas
Definition: A visual framework for describing a company’s value proposition, infrastructure, customers, and finances.

Term: Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
Definition: A product with just enough features to be usable by early customers who can then provide feedback for future development.

Term: Agile Methodology
Definition: A project management approach commonly used in software development that emphasizes iterative progress, collaboration, and flexibility.

Term: Value Proposition
Definition: A statement that explains how a product solves a problem, delivers specific benefits, and why it is better than alternatives.
